Research

Research interests, active projects, and collaborations

Research Overview

My research lies at the intersection of Machine Learning, Data Mining, Big Data Analytics, and Complex Networks. I am particularly interested in designing scalable, principled algorithms for discovering meaningful patterns and structures in large, heterogeneous, and evolving datasets — and in understanding how complex systems such as social and information networks form, grow, and behave over time. My work combines theoretical foundations with practical, data-driven applications.

Research Areas

🤖

Machine Learning

Developing and analyzing machine learning algorithms — from classical models to modern deep learning architectures — with a focus on robustness, scalability, and applicability to real-world, large-scale data.

Supervised & Unsupervised Learning Deep Learning Representation Learning Model Evaluation
🔍

Data Mining

Extracting meaningful patterns and knowledge from large-scale, heterogeneous datasets. Developing scalable algorithms for frequent pattern mining, sequential pattern mining, classification, clustering, and anomaly detection.

Pattern Mining Association Rules Clustering Anomaly Detection
📊

Big Data Analytics

Designing efficient methods, pipelines, and tools for processing, analyzing, and extracting actionable insights from high-volume, high-velocity, and high-variety data sources using distributed and scalable computing frameworks.

Distributed Computing Scalable Algorithms Data Pipelines Stream Processing
🌐

Complex Networks

Studying the structure, dynamics, and evolution of complex networks — including social, information, and biological networks — through community detection, link analysis, influence propagation, and network modeling.

Social Network Analysis Community Detection Graph Mining Network Dynamics
